With growing demands of portable and wearable devices, it has become important to develop miniaturized, low-power chips. Thus, my current research is focussed in areas like digital electronics, digital signal processing, VLSI circuits and low-power designs. I now seek to integrate the principles of electrical engineering and computer science with biomedical and neuroscience. I want to develop power efficient, miniaturized, wearable or implantable biomedical devices for clinical application to enhance healthcare and quality of life for people worldwide. These biomedical devices may find their usage in improving mental health; diagnosing and treating neurological disorders; and helping regain mobility by means of neuroprosthetics and brain-machine interfaces.
